Pollution Hotspots — Tunisia
🏭

Gabès — Chemical Coast

Home to Tunisia's most toxic industrial complex. Phosphate processing has devastated marine life in the Gulf of Gabès, a UNESCO-recognized site.

🌊

Sfax — Plastic Shore

Industrial and domestic waste converges at Tunisia's second city. Sfax's beaches regularly fail quality standards.

🚗

Tunis — Urban Haze

The capital chokes under traffic emissions and construction dust. PM2.5 levels regularly hit 7× the WHO safe threshold.

⛏️

Gafsa — Mining Scars

Decades of phosphate extraction have left polluted water tables and degraded land across the interior.

What
We
Can Do

Pollution is not inevitable. Concrete policy, technology, and individual action can reverse the damage — if we start now.

01
☀️

Renewable Energy Transition

Tunisia has exceptional solar and wind resources. A rapid transition away from fossil fuels — especially in electricity generation — would cut urban air pollution by an estimated 35% within a decade. The government's Tunisian Solar Plan targets 30% renewable energy by 2030.

02
🚌

Green Transport

Electrify public bus fleets, expand the Tunis metro, and implement vehicle emission standards. Cities that have done this saw a 25%+ reduction in roadside NO₂.

03
♻️

Waste Management

Build modern sorting facilities, ban single-use plastics, and create circular economy incentives. Morocco's plastic bag ban reduced coastal plastic by 40%.

04
🌱

Agroecology

Transition farmers to organic and regenerative practices. Reduces chemical runoff and sequesters carbon in soil — a dual climate-pollution solution.

05
⚖️

Industrial Accountability

Enforce existing environmental laws. Create a real-time air quality monitoring network. Fine industrial violators and publish their data publicly.
